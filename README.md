I am a Ph.D. candidate in Decision Sciences at the Indian Institute of Management Bangalore, where my work focuses on developing and applying advanced statistical frameworks to track complex, dynamic processes across space and time. My research interests lie at the intersection of applied statistics, life-course analysis, and spatial modelling. Methodologically, I am dedicated to constructing rigorous, data-driven frameworks that translate high-dimensional longitudinal datasets into predictive models and actionable structural insights. Driven by rational curiosity and a commitment to evidence-based scholarship, my overarching objective is to bridge computational methodologies with critical public policy and health challenges, scaling up my research to meet the standards of the world’s premier academic institutions. 
Currently, I am working on the spatio-temporal modelling of Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR), an increasingly critical domain that demands highly sophisticated statistical surveillance and regional tracking frameworks. My technical approach leverages heavy spatiotemporal and time-series forecasting to decode localised and macro-level dissemination pathways. In my previous appointments, including extensive data-driven roles at the Centre for Public Policy (IIMB) and the University of Western Australia Business School, I engineered analytical pipelines to manage and clean massive, complex datasets, such as the CMIE Consumer Pyramids Household Survey (CPHS), for panel data modelling. This specialised training in causal inference, multi-city survey analysis, and micro-econometric identification provides the foundation for my empirical capabilities. 
To execute these complex methodologies, I maintain a robust computational toolkit, with expert proficiency in Stata and LaTeX, alongside a working command of R, MATLAB, Python, SAS, and specialised spatial mapping suites such as GeoDA and ArcGIS. My near-term trajectory focuses on refining these advanced mathematical and statistical models within decision sciences to address large-scale social vulnerabilities, health shocks, and spatial disparities. Looking forward, I aim to establish strong collaborative networks with global statisticians and decision scientists, pursuing postdoctoral and institutional research roles within premier global think tanks and universities to build international discourse around data-driven societal change.
